# Develop Creative Brief

Translate an idea into an executable agreement. Keep subjective language testable through examples and exclusions.

## Process

1. Define the business or editorial objective and the audience action or feeling.
2. List every deliverable with quantity, orientation, aspect ratio, crop-safe area, resolution, file type, and channel.
3. State the single key message, supporting messages, mandatory elements, and prohibited elements.
4. Specify visual direction: tone, palette, contrast, lighting, composition, casting, wardrobe, environment, motion, retouching, and brand rules.
5. Record usage: owner/licensee, media, territory, duration, exclusivity, paid advertising, third-party distribution, and embargo.
6. Document budget, timing, location, talent, technical, accessibility, sustainability, and legal constraints.
7. Name the decision maker, reviewers, feedback rounds, decision deadlines, and definition of approval.

## Output

Return: project summary; objective; audience; message hierarchy; deliverables matrix; visual direction; references and anti-references; constraints; usage; success criteria; stakeholders/RACI; milestones; assumptions; open decisions.

Do not invent brand requirements, legal usage rights, performance claims, or deliverable counts. Mark missing facts clearly.
