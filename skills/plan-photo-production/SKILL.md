# Plan Photo Production

Build one source of truth and delegate each workstream to the matching specialist skill.

## Intake

Capture: shoot type, objectives, deliverables and channels; date and location; client/approver; audience; creative direction; team scale; talent; budget ceiling; usage/territory/term; deadlines; camera/lighting constraints; accessibility and safety needs.

Do not block on noncritical gaps. Mark them `TBD`, assign an owner, and set a due date.

## Workflow

1. Use `$develop-creative-brief` to lock purpose, audience, message, deliverables, visual guardrails, and approval chain.
2. Use `$build-photo-mood-board` to translate the brief into visual references with annotated intent.
3. Use `$scout-photo-location` to compare creative fit, light, access, permits, safety, weather, and logistics.
4. Use `$create-photo-shot-list` to map every required asset to a setup, talent/look, orientation, and priority.
5. Use `$coordinate-talent-crew` to build the roster, responsibilities, bookings, contacts, and dependencies.
6. Use `$manage-shoot-budget` to create estimate, contingency, actuals, and approval thresholds.
7. Use `$plan-photo-equipment` and `$manage-wardrobe-props` for gear and physical-asset manifests.
8. Use `$check-shoot-permits-releases` before confirming public/private locations or recognizable people/property.
9. Use `$build-photo-call-sheet` only after the shot list, location, crew, and travel assumptions stabilize.
10. Call `validate_shoot_readiness`; resolve blockers before distributing the production book.

## Production book

Deliver these sections in order:

- One-page creative brief and approval chain
- Mood-board direction and visual rules
- Location decision plus backup and weather trigger
- Numbered shot list grouped by setup
- Crew/talent roster and responsibilities
- Call sheet with timezone, travel, parking, meals, safety and contacts
- Equipment, wardrobe and prop manifests
- Budget summary, contingency and open purchase orders
- Permit, insurance and release status
- Risk register, decision deadlines and plan B

Protect personal contact details. Provide the full contact sheet only to authorized production members.

## Quality gate

Confirm every must-have deliverable maps to a shot; schedule math fits the day; overtime and meal rules are considered; location and releases are verified; weather and backup triggers are explicit; budget includes contingency; owners exist for all TBDs.
