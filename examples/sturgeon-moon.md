# Sturgeon Moon Over Manhattan

An example field plan for photographing the full Sturgeon Moon from northern New Jersey on August 9, 2025. Times and directions below come from the supplied planning notes and are retained as a historical example; recalculate astronomy and verify weather, access, closures, and permit rules before reusing this plan for another date.

## Objective

Capture the full moon rising behind or near the Manhattan skyline, then make tighter moon-detail frames after it clears the horizon haze.

Primary deliverables:

- Wide environmental image with the moon, skyline, and New York Harbor
- Telephoto skyline compression with the moon appearing large against buildings
- Clean exposed-for-the-moon detail frame
- Short sequence showing the moon's movement after moonrise

## Location plan

### Primary: Liberty State Park, Jersey City

**Best window:** 7:30–9:30 PM EDT

**Direction:** Establish an unobstructed sightline toward 109–110° before moonrise.

Suggested scouting areas:

- Open waterfront near Empty Sky Memorial
- Waterfront paths in the Liberty Science Center side of the park

Creative strengths:

- Manhattan skyline, harbor, and Statue of Liberty context
- Water reflections and blue-hour color around moonrise
- Flat ground that simplifies tripod setup and repositioning

Constraints to verify:

- Current park and parking hours, including which waterfront areas remain accessible after sunset
- Tripod or commercial-photography restrictions
- Construction, events, security closures, and waterfront barriers
- Wind exposure along the harbor

### Alternate: Eagle Rock Reservation, West Orange

**Best window:** 7:30–10:00 PM EDT

**Direction:** Use the overlook's east-southeast view to follow the moon from rise into the evening sky.

Suggested scouting area:

- 9/11 Memorial overlook and adjacent viewing area

Creative strengths:

- Elevated, wide view over the New York metropolitan area
- More room to follow the moon's path after it rises
- Convenient equipment access from nearby parking

Constraints to verify:

- Current reservation and parking hours
- Tree growth, haze, and foreground obstructions along the intended bearing
- Crowding around the overlook and safe tripod placement
- Stronger wind and cooler conditions at the ridge

## Go/no-go decision

Make the location decision by 4:00 PM EDT.

Choose Liberty State Park when:

- The eastern horizon is clear enough for a low moonrise
- Harbor wind is manageable for long lenses
- Skyline and waterfront access are confirmed

Choose Eagle Rock when:

- The waterfront is closed, crowded, or excessively windy
- A higher, broader view offers better visibility above low haze
- The priority shifts from reflections to an elevated skyline panorama

Postpone or change the concept when the eastern horizon has opaque cloud, heavy smoke or haze hides the skyline, lightning is possible, or official access cannot be confirmed.

## Camera plan

Bring two focal-length options if available:

- **24–70 mm:** harbor, skyline, and environmental compositions
- **200–600 mm:** compressed skyline alignments and moon detail

Starting points:

| Setup | Mode | Aperture | Shutter | ISO | Notes |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Blue-hour skyline | Manual | f/8 | 1/4–2 s | 100–400 | Bracket for city lights; check wind vibration |
| Moon with skyline | Manual | f/8 | 1/60–1/250 s | 200–800 | Expose to retain lunar detail; bracket the foreground separately |
| Moon detail | Manual | f/8–f/11 | 1/125–1/500 s | 100–400 | Focus manually using magnified live view |
| Motion sequence | Manual | f/8 | 1/125 s or faster | Auto ISO capped as needed | Lock framing and interval consistently |

The moon and skyline can exceed a single exposure's dynamic range. Capture bracketed frames without moving the tripod and keep a natural-looking single-exposure option for comparison.

## Shot list

1. **Must have — blue-hour establishing frame:** Skyline and harbor before moonrise.
2. **Must have — first visible moon:** Long-lens frame as the lunar disk clears haze or the skyline.
3. **Must have — compressed alignment:** Moon placed beside or above a recognizable skyline feature.
4. **Must have — exposed-for-moon detail:** Preserve crater and maria texture without clipping highlights.
5. **Should have — water reflection:** Wider frame if wind and surface conditions cooperate.
6. **Should have — moonrise sequence:** Identical framing at regular intervals for a later composite.
7. **Could have — people in context:** Small silhouettes observing the moon, avoiding identifiable close portraits without permission.

## Risks and mitigations

- **Wrong alignment:** Scout with a moon-planning app from the exact tripod position and leave room to move laterally.
- **Horizon haze:** Continue shooting as the moon climbs; switch emphasis to detail frames if the skyline is obscured.
- **Blown lunar highlights:** Use manual exposure, watch the histogram, and bracket toward faster shutter speeds.
- **Tripod vibration:** Lower the center column, use a lens collar, disable stabilization when appropriate, and shield the setup from gusts.
- **Crowds:** Arrive early, keep paths clear, and use a compact footprint.
- **Waterfront or overlook hazards:** Stay behind barriers, avoid unlit edges, and do not relocate while looking through the camera.
- **Access uncertainty:** Confirm official hours and closures on the shoot day; never rely on informal reports that an area remains open late.
